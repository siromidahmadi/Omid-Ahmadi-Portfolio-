Week 2 · Purpose, Values & Leadership Voice

The Golden Circle.

Purpose-driven leaders reverse the usual order. They start with WHY — the belief that drives everything — then build outward to HOW they lead, and finally WHAT they deliver. This is my leadership identity from the inside out.

WHY
Purpose
WHY
What drives me to lead
I believe technology should serve people — not just function. What drives me is the gap between what communities need and what currently exists. Whether it's a satellite signal dropping during a storm, or a neighborhood lacking the data to advocate for itself, I want to be part of closing that gap.
Technology that reaches millions should be built with resilience and equity in mind
Communities deserve data, tools, and leadership that reflect their real conditions
I lead because the work matters — not just to deliver results, but to create systems that endure
HOW
Values & Approach
HOW
How I lead in practice
I lead by listening first, then building. I bring structured thinking — project charters, quality frameworks, testing rigor — into environments that move fast and operate under pressure. My values show up most clearly when things are unclear.
Clarity under pressure: I communicate directly, document decisions, and keep teams oriented when priorities shift
Cross-functional trust: I collaborate across engineering, community, planning, and design without losing technical integrity
Quality as a human value: Whether testing software or planning a corridor, I treat thoroughness as a form of respect for the people who depend on the outcome
WHAT
Outcomes & Contribution
WHAT
What I consistently deliver
I deliver projects that ship — and stay shipped. From a 2024 award-winning satellite resiliency feature reaching millions, to a community-scale digital twin of the Baldwin Hills Corridor, I bring technical depth and project leadership to work that matters.
Resilient systems that perform under real-world conditions and scale to large audiences
Project clarity: structured documentation, stakeholder alignment, and on-time delivery
Teams and communities can expect honest communication, follow-through, and work that reflects their needs

Position Statement

Who I am as a leader.

I earned my Facilities Management Certificate, where I completed projects such as a project charter, work breakdown structure (WBS), closeout evaluation, and stakeholder presentation.

That program prepared me to plan, execute, and deliver projects, communicate with cross-functional stakeholders across engineering, operations, and community teams, and follow structured quality and safety standards.

Leadership today means I can facilitate decisions and communicate clearly even when facing ambiguity, shifting priorities, time pressure, or limited authority.

In this course, I want to strengthen my ability to lead projects and initiatives creatively so I can move from individual contributor to someone who drives team outcomes and creates lasting impact.

My "why" right now is to grow into a role where my technical skills are matched by my leadership presence — and where I can lead work that genuinely matters to the communities I serve.
